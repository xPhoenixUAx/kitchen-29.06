Before You Hire

Good Questions Make Better Remodel Conversations

When a provider contacts you, use the conversation to understand their fit for your project. A clear scope protects both the homeowner and the provider.

  • Ask for license and insurance details required for the work in your location.
  • Request a written scope that lists materials, labor, exclusions, payment schedule, and warranty terms.
  • Confirm lead times for cabinets, countertops, inspections, and specialty trades before scheduling demolition.
  • Prepare photos, measurements, appliance model numbers, and a list of must-have changes.
Homeowner FAQ

Common Questions Before Requesting Help

These answers keep the aggregator relationship clear before you submit your kitchen remodeling inquiry.

Is this service free for homeowners?

Yes. The site is a free connection service for homeowners. Independent providers may offer estimates, consultations, or paid services according to their own business practices.

Can one request cover multiple kitchen services?

Yes. If your project includes cabinets, counters, flooring, tile, fixtures, or lighting, describe everything in the form so the request can be routed more intelligently.

Are the providers employees of this site?

No. Providers are independent businesses. This site does not employ them, supervise their work, or guarantee the result of any project.

What should I verify before hiring?

Verify licensing, insurance, references, written scope, permits, payment terms, warranty language, and who is responsible for each trade before any work begins.
