Tile Project Details

  • Wall surface review, old backsplash removal, drywall repair, and substrate preparation.
  • Tile layout, pattern planning, accent areas, range feature walls, and open-edge solutions.

  • Grout color, sealant, caulk lines, edge trim, cleaning, curing time, and maintenance.

Tile Layout Detail

Backsplash Tile Is a Small Area With Many Visible Decisions

Backsplash work depends on wall prep, tile layout, outlet cuts, edge trim, grout, cabinet ends, windows, hood areas, and countertop alignment. Clear photos and material notes help a provider understand whether the job is simple or detail-heavy.

Layout plan

Pattern start point, cuts near cabinets, corners, open ends, windows, outlets, and range features.

Surface prep

Old tile removal, drywall repair, flattening, primer, substrate suitability, and dust control.

Finish choices

Grout color, grout width, edge trim, caulk lines, sealers, and cleaning instructions.

Coordination

Under-cabinet lights, switch plates, hood trim, shelves, countertops, and curing time.

Before You Speak With a Provider

  • Share photos straight on from each backsplash wall.
  • Note tile size, pattern preference, and whether tile is already purchased.
  • Ask how outlets will be extended and finished after tile thickness is added.
