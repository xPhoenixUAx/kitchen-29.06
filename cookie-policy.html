Cookie Summary

Simple, Operational Use

Cookies may support basic site operation, security, form behavior, preference handling, and performance measurement. Advertising or analytics tools should only be active when specifically configured.

Your choice: You can control cookies through your browser settings. Some site features may not work as intended if certain cookies are blocked.

1. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your browser or device by websites you visit. Similar technologies may include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, scripts, log files, or device identifiers. These technologies can help websites remember preferences, process forms, measure performance, and protect against misuse.

2. How This Website May Use Cookies

This website may use cookies or similar technologies to operate pages, maintain basic functionality, support form submissions, remember limited preferences, improve loading behavior, detect spam or abuse, understand general site usage, and maintain security logs. These uses support the website as a free homeowner connection service.

3. Strictly Necessary Technologies

Some technologies may be necessary for the website to load, display correctly, process a request, protect against automated abuse, or maintain basic security. These technologies are typically essential to provide the site and may not be available for separate opt-out through the website itself.

4. Performance and Analytics

If analytics tools are configured, they may help us understand page visits, traffic sources, device types, interaction patterns, form performance, and general site reliability. Analytics data is generally used in aggregate to improve the website and intake process.

No specific analytics or advertising tracking ID should be assumed active unless it has actually been configured on the site.

This website should not contain advertising or remarketing tags unless specific tracking IDs are provided, approved, and implemented. Their practices are governed by their own policies, not this Cookie Policy.

7. Form and Communication Functionality

Cookies or local browser storage may support forms, validation, success messages, spam prevention, or temporary site behavior. If these technologies are disabled, a request form may fail to submit, display incorrectly, or lose information entered before submission.

8. Browser Controls

Most browsers allow you to block, delete, or limit cookies. Browser settings vary, so review your browser's help section for instructions. Blocking all cookies may affect site functionality. Clearing cookies may remove saved preferences or reset session behavior.

9. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer Do Not Track or similar signals. Because there is no single industry standard for responding to these signals, this website may not respond to them in a uniform way. You can still use browser cookie controls and privacy settings.
